Prof. Dr. med. Olivio Donati
The analysis of your brain scan shows that the volume of your thalamus is slightly above the typical reference range. The thalamus is a key structure in the centre of the brain that acts as a relay station for information. A value that is minimally outside the standard range can be a normal variation for an individual and is not necessarily a cause for concern. Your MRI also identified an enlarged Virchow-Robin space. These are normal, fluid-filled channels that surround small blood vessels in the brain. It is very common to see them on an MRI, and this finding is considered a descriptive observation of your brain's anatomy. We also noted that the volumes of several other brain regions, such as your cortical gray matter, are within the expected range but appear smaller compared to your previous scan. Volume measurements can vary between scans for a number of reasons. Given these combined observations, continued monitoring of these volumes in a future scan is advisable to understand any potential trend over the long term. Supporting your brain health through regular physical activity, a balanced diet, and managing stress is always beneficial.

Prof. Dr. med. Olivio Donati
The MRI of your head revealed fluid within the mastoid air cells on both sides. The mastoid is a part of the skull bone located behind your ear, and it contains small, honeycomb-like air cells that are normally filled with air. These air cells are connected to your upper respiratory system. Fluid in this area can be a sign of inflammation or a blockage of the drainage pathways, which may be related to a common cold, allergies, or sinus issues. This is a minor observation and often does not cause any symptoms. The fluid may clear up on its own as the inflammation resolves. Should you experience symptoms like ear pain, a feeling of fullness in the ear, or changes in your hearing, a consultation with your primary care doctor would be a sensible next step. For now, this finding does not require any specific follow-up.

Prof. Dr. med. Olivio Donati
Your blood test results provide a good overview of your kidney function. The analysis from October 2024 showed an estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate (eGFR) of 82 ml/min/1.73m. The eGFR is a measure of how well your kidneys filter waste from the blood. A value of 82 indicates a mild reduction in the filtration rate. However, it is important to place this value in context. The eGFR is calculated from your creatinine level, which is a waste product from muscle activity. Levels can fluctuate due to factors like your muscle mass, intense physical activity, or hydration status. For a man of your age, a value in this range is often not a cause for concern. It is reassuring that your most recent creatinine measurement from September 2025 has improved and is well within the normal range. This indicates that your kidney filtration rate is currently at a very good level. All other values related to your kidney health, such as urea and your electrolytes like sodium and potassium, are also in the normal range, which further supports the finding of healthy kidney function. To continue supporting your kidney health, we recommend ensuring you stay well-hydrated.

Prof. Dr. med. Olivio Donati
Your examination revealed some age-related changes in your spine. In your lower back, at the level L4/L5, one of the discs that cushions your vertebrae is bulging, particularly towards the left side. This is causing a moderate narrowing of the spinal canal, the main channel for your nerves, and also of the smaller opening where a nerve root exits on the left. This narrowing can sometimes put pressure on the nerve, which may lead to symptoms like back pain or pain, numbness, or tingling sensations radiating down your left leg. Similar, but milder, changes were found in your neck. There are disc bulges at several levels (C3-C6), causing a slight narrowing of the spinal canal at the C5/C6 level. We also noted some mild wear-and-tear arthritis in the small joints of your spine, which is common with these types of disc changes. On a very positive note, the analysis of your back muscles shows that they are of excellent quality. The amount of fat within your deep core muscles, such as the Psoas, Multifidus, and Erector spinae, is very low. This is a significant advantage, as strong, healthy muscles provide crucial support and stability for your spine. This muscular corset can help to protect your spine from further strain and may reduce the impact of the structural changes we have seen. To proactively manage your long-term spinal health, it is highly beneficial to continue focusing on maintaining this excellent muscle strength, particularly in your core. Paying attention to good posture and using proper lifting techniques can also help to prevent placing unnecessary stress on the discs. Please be mindful of any new or worsening symptoms in your arms or legs and consult with your doctor if they arise.

Prof. Dr. med. Olivio Donati
Your thyroid function was assessed by measuring the Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone, or TSH. Your current TSH level is 3.2 mU/l, which is within the standard reference range. TSH is a hormone from your brain that directs your thyroid gland's activity, which in turn regulates your body's metabolism. We did notice that this value has increased compared to your previous measurement of 1.92 mU/l. While your current level is still in the normal range, this upward trend can be an early indicator that your thyroid gland is working harder to produce the necessary hormones. It is important to know that TSH levels can fluctuate due to various factors, including time of day and stress. From a preventive standpoint, we recommend monitoring this development. A follow-up blood test in about 6 to 12 months would be sensible to check the TSH level again and to see if a trend emerges. To support optimal thyroid function, you can pay attention to a diet that includes sufficient iodine and selenium.
